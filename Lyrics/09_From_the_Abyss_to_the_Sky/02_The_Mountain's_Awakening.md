# The Mountain's Awakening

**Album:** From the Abyss to the Sky  
**Track:** 02  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

---

She lay, an ancient seabed, vast, unknown,
beneath the breathing ocean, slow and dreaming.
The shells sank silver down, like soft snow sown,
and silence reigned there, primal, past all seeming.

The ages passed. The earth then shook and reared,
the dark deep waters trembled, stirred, and shivered.
Up through the mass of seas, from fire, appeared
her summits, to the light at last delivered.

The granite darkened, bared to every blast,
the rock reached slowly skyward, climbing, growing.
The storms boomed down the steep-cut ridges, vast,
and in her, her own nature woke, full-flowing.

The pine and fir then clothed her, dark and tall,
and wild grass spread across her slopes, ascending.
The crags drank in the voices, one and all —
the countless gulls' sad cries, the still air rending.

Upon the peaks an untouched stillness sleeps,
the snow gleams, frozen into waves and ridges.
The starlit air lies still across the steeps,
above the ancient glaciers' icy bridges.

And in her depths the surf still booms and churns,
an echo from the buried years, returning.
Deep in her heart another cosmos turns —
the sea's old secrets, held since her first burning.

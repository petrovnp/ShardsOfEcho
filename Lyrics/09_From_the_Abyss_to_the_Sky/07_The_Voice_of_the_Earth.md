# The Voice of the Earth

**Album:** From the Abyss to the Sky  
**Track:** 07  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

---

I am the silence older than all gales.
I am the depth that has no bound or measure.
My fire once forged the face of worlds and vales;
in me the first song sang of life, of treasure.

With storms I rend the heavy vault of sky;
with waves I wear the changeless granite down.
The ancient subterranean heat won't die;
my polar night burns bright, a glowing crown.

I raised the mountain ridges to the clouds
and sank the floor of ocean depths untold.
I recall all — from glaciers' first cold shrouds
to warm streams, and to human hearths of gold.

But I am not just thunder, stone, and might.
My dawn ascends above the seas, slow-glowing;
a thousand forests murmur in my sight,
the prairie blazes, wild with bright flowers growing.

The deserts breathe a golden haze and fume;
the summits drowse in icy crowns, unbroken;
and waterfalls, through dense and dark, resume
their roar in gorges, narrow, deep, unspoken.

In me, no thing is lost without a trace.
The fiery mountain cools and turns to stone;
the quiet water wears away its face,
and ash shall rise as green grass, fully grown.

I am the storm and calm, the dark, light's morn;
I am the ice and heat, the ash, perfection;
all that has gone returns to me, reborn.
I am the round forever — death, resurrection.

And I, the silence older than all gales,
and I, the depth that has no bound or measure,
and I, the round of ash and flowering vales —
all this am I. The Earth. Who sang this treasure.

# The Storm

**Album:** From the Abyss to the Sky  
**Track:** 04  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

---

The burning heat hung frozen on the plain.
The birds fell mute in dense and stifling air.
The world lay wrapped in one thick, heavy skein,
in dusty, sultry half-sleep, breathless, bare.

But far away the sky grew dark and grim.
A sudden gust bent grass in waves unending.
The first low thunder grumbled at the rim,
and storm clouds crept, their dark mass slow-extending.

It flashes! Thunder cracks across the hills!
The rain comes down — torrential, thick, and fast!
The water streams toward the fields in rills,
and leaping drops come drumming, wild and vast!

The sky flares up with bursts of silver light,
the streams run winding through the rocks and stone.
The trees bend low, the winds lash left and right,
the thunder swells — more furious, fully grown.

One last far peal — now duller, fainter, low...
and soft the echo fades on fields below.

The gust dies down. The veil grows thin and clears.
The downpour fades behind some distant hill.
The washed world breathes — a hush, as calm appears,
and breathes of rain, and earth, and grass, and chill.

The cool air woke and stirred across the ground.
The birds broke into song in ringing height.
The world now glints with raindrop-dew around,
awoken into primal, pure, clean light.

The cool air woke and stirred across the ground...
the birds now singing...
...the world, alive.

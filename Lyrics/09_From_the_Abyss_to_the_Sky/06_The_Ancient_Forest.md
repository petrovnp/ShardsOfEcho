# The Ancient Forest

The sunset settles on the mountain crests,
and evening spreads its shadows on the ground.
Below, a boundless forest, blackening, rests,
in ageless half-light, motionless, profound.

I stepped beneath the canopy of night.
The dark closed in — no sound, no spark, no flame.
Here stands a silence older than all sight,
and the forest breathed, as if it knew I came.

Here hungry roots bit deep into the loam,
the trunks rose up to crowns beyond all seeming;
a hoary fog enwrapped the silence, home
to nothing but the half-asleep wood's dreaming.

But something stirred and trembled in the boughs,
a shadow darted somewhere at my back.
The alien stares of beasts — a hundred brows —
seeped darkness from the frozen thicket black.

A dead, dry whisper swallowed every thought;
the path dissolved; the gloom grew thick and vast.
A clammy fear had brought my breath to naught;
and night lay down with grave-like weight, at last.

But I did not run. I stilled myself. I wait.

The fear released its grip; my heart grew bold.
The wood made no threat — watchful, it lay still.
It kept what time had carried off of old;
it spoke to me; I could not grasp its will.

The trees drew back, and now the rising moon
bathed all the silent forest in its light.
A glade, the stars, the heaven's depth, and soon
the whisper of the crowns — a choir of night!

I AM this wood! I am its branches' breath!
My voice rings in the crowns, the wind set free!
From deep abyss to deathless fires, past death —
I AM this wood! And this wood lives in me!

And now the shadows softened, mild and light.
A warm nocturnal peace spread out, my own.
No fear, no doubt, no longing in the night;
and through the wood I walk, no more alone.

The sunset settles on the mountain crests,
and evening spreads its shadows on the ground.
Below, a boundless forest, blackening, rests,
and the forest lives... in me...

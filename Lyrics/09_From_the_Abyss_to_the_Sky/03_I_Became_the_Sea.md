# I Became the Sea

**Album:** From the Abyss to the Sky  
**Track:** 03  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

From under stones, from under grey snow, welling,
where silence never knew a single sound,
I rose, as clear as sky — past words, past telling,
the finest chime in forests, mute, profound.

Not knowing earth — so new it was, so gleaming,
not telling bank from bed, nor depth from height,
I flowed on moss and naked stone, still dreaming,
a thread of crystal, lone in silent night.

The stony gorge grew tight, too cramped, confining,
and I broke loose — all fury, wild, and fleet!
Through roar and foam and white-hot seething, shining,
I plunged, a silver torrent, swift, complete.

Each shore is home to me — but for a breath;
no gorge, no rapid is my final border.
I hold the current and the spring beneath;
I know I must not stop — that is my order.

The mighty rapids now lie far behind me,
I've spread across the warm and drowsy meadows.
Here all grew still and deep, and song would find me,
and freely move my floods through softened shadows.

Each shore is home to me — but for a breath;
the valley's peace, as well, is not my border.
I keep its parting gleam, held deep beneath;
I know I must not stop — that is my order.

A salty wind; a wing's lone silhouette;
the channel thins, the streams grow wide and braiding.
With open sea I meet the dawn, and yet
the far horizon spreads, immense and fading.

From under stones, from under grey snow, welling,
where silence never knew a single sound.
I rose, as clear as sky — past words, past telling...
and I became the sea...

Each shore is home to me — but for a breath;
and even sea, as well, is not my border.
I keep the spring's first face within my depth;
I know I must not stop — that is my order.

Within me — source, and waterfall, and way;
I cannot... cannot ever stop, or stay.

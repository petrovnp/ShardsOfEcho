# Through the Worlds

**Album:** The Long Way Home  
**Track:** 06  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

---

The sunset stained the rooftops past the pane,
a grief weighs on my heart, a silence deep.
Alone within the empty house I've lain,
yet still your image fills the nights I sleep.

You were my light in darkest hours of pain,
warm breath within the twilight of my grief.
Without a word, you understood again,
and met each evening with me, my relief.

You walked the starry road and traveled far,
and left me but the light of memory's flame.
And in those reaches made your home, a star
almost beyond my conscious mind's last claim.

One path runs out to thousand worlds, and splits,
and branches like the roots of all that's spun.
Along it not a single trace persists —
lost in the silent dust, your trail undone.

I guard my memory like a tiny flame —
this thread alone still binds me close to you.
And it will lead me to the source, my aim,
when I, too, walk the road I must pursue.

I'll find you yet — I'll walk the whole long road
through all my countless rebirths, life on life.
That tiny flame in me shall not be slowed
nor snuffed by winds of bitter doubt and strife.

The moon now shines beyond the windowpane,
a silence settles soft upon my heart.
I watch the stars as night begins to wane,
and still your image fills my every part.

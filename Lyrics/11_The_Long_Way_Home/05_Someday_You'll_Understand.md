# Someday You'll Understand

Someday you'll see the truth, and stand there shaken:
that you have lived all wrong, not as you should,
that dreams you chased are lost now, dead, forsaken,
and this great weight will stay on you for good.

An unseen shadow weighs upon your heart,
from all the years you rushed and threw away.
You raced ahead, and let the hours depart,
then turned — the mirrors empty, cold, and grey.

You climbed toward the top — headlong, with fire,
not seeing how the swift years slipped and fled.
You used up people, climbing ever higher,
and lost them — silent, gone, forever dead.

An unseen shadow weighs upon your heart,
from all the years you rushed and threw away.
You raced ahead, and let the hours depart,
then turned — the mirrors empty, cold, and grey.

And now you stand alone upon the edge,
where every road leads outward into void,
no goal ahead, no strength, no vow, no pledge,
no soul to share the life you've left destroyed.

And night now grips your throat before you sleep,
and in the dark the voices rise and swell
of those you left in secret, silent, deep,
whose eyes you never dared to meet — farewell.

But somewhere in the hush there still remains
a flame you never managed to put out,
and if your heart still beats within your veins —
it's not too late to turn yourself about.
It's not... too late... to turn yourself about.

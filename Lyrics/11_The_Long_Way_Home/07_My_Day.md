# My Day

The dawn crept in on soft paws through the house,
the old framed photographs woke on the wall.
Its gentle ray then brushed my fur — a mouse
of light — and broke my deepest dream of all.

I stretched, I arched my back, I rose up light,
to rouse my world, to bid the morning break.
I called them softly — tender, low, and bright.
I know for sure — it's time, now, to awake.

And now at last they're rising, one by one.
Their voices grow more lively, warm, and clear.
The smell of coffee brings its cozy sun.
And I can close my eyes again, right here.

And now they've gathered up, about to go.
The warm touch of a hand upon my head...
I have no time to give one glance — and so —
the door has shut, the locks have clicked instead.

So slowly now this long, slow day goes by.
I listened, climbed up to the windowsill.
Outside, the shadows lengthened toward the sky.
I watch the world go by, and sit stock-still.

I know at once the well-known, longed-for tread.
Unhurried, calm, I go to meet them there.
Inside, I breathe a quiet joy, unsaid.
They long to scoop me up with loving care.

We dined together, as we always do.
From weighty tasks I try to lure them away.
I slipped on past, and hooked a wire or two —
hoping to make them notice me, and play.

I nestle close beside them, soft and deep,
and watch the movements of their tired eyes.
My purring — tenderness, and gentle sleep.
A healing melody — our dearest ties.

And then our eyes at last, at last, had met —
aware, and deep, and lingering, and true.
The moment's brief, the seconds flew, and yet
I felt so free, so light, so wholly new.

Outside the world has fallen wholly dark,
and silence wraps the house in stillness, deep.
A little time is ours — ours now to mark —
to breathe as one. One breath. And one shared sleep.

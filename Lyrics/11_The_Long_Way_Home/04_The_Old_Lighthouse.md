# The Old Lighthouse

**Written:** 2026

High on the cliffs, above the foaming spray,
where howling winds drive cloud and storm and wrack,
there stands a lighthouse — weathered, worn, and grey,
forsaken, lost to ages, turned to black.

The sunset died; the gloom enclosed the world;
the sea alone beat dully on the stone.
Then all at once — a flame! The light unfurled,
a beam tore through the dark. The lighthouse shone.

But then a storm — ferocious, icy-cold —
came thundering, and reared a black wave high,
and lashed the shore with blind waves, uncontrolled —
the granite shuddered, gave a heavy cry.

And somewhere out in pitch-black, blinding night,
a frigate drove toward reefs. Death lay in wait.
No star, no sign, no glimmer of a light —
a moment more, the sea would seal its fate.

A light broke through! Alive, it flickered, swept
across the mast, the torn and tattered sail.
The frigate woke, a new course now it kept —
and trusted unknown fires through the gale.

The ship sailed on and vanished far from sight.
The storm grew still. The dawn began to break.
And on the cliff, atop that ancient height,
the lonely light went dark — a rest to take.

High on the cliffs, above the foaming spray,
where howling winds drive cloud and storm and wrack,
there stands a lighthouse — weathered, worn, and grey,
forsaken, lost to ages, turned to black...

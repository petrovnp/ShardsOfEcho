# The Autumn Pass

**Album:** The Long Way Home  
**Track:** 02  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

---

The autumn woods are dressed in burning gold,
the air is crystal, edged with early frost.
A path climbs upward toward the snows of old,
and one last leaf turns slowly, drifting, lost.

I walk ahead; my soul is light and free.
A brook rings out among the mossy stone.
Far off, a ridge dissolves into the sea
of cloud; the silent pass stands still, alone.

The climb grows steep. The brook has fallen still.
The shapes all blur; the colours drain and grey.
The fog creeps in, more dense, with deepening chill,
and all the world turns pale, and fades away.

A pale, thick murk where clinging shadows lay
had filled it all — no daylight struggled free.
Through silence on the blind road I make way —
here even time itself won't come for me.

The white wall thins at last, and breaks, and clears.
A final step — the clouds lie at my feet.
Bare rock and wind; the open distance nears,
the dome of heaven, high and clear, complete.

My breath has stopped. I stand, struck to the core.
Such open space — no soul could hold it all.
And now, it seems, I understand, far more
than ever, why this stillness was my call.

My gaze slips out beyond the ridge, takes flight.
The hills lie deep in velvet grass, serene.
A river gleams, full-flowing, free and bright —
and all this living country, lush and green.

I tread the gentler slope as it descends,
through warm air thick with pine and thyme and balm;
a tender birdsong rises, breaks, and blends —
here all is changed; in me there's now a calm.

I sat upon a rock and traced the climb,
the snowy trail across the pass of stone.
I simply walked, not knowing why, in time —
the one I sought was me, at last made known.

# The Beast at World's Edge

**Album:** Keepers of Time  
**Track:** 02  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026  
**© 2026 Nickolay Petrov. All rights reserved.**

---

The world's far edge. Beyond — no land, no star.
Here slumbers time. A dead and final bound.
A giant beast, like stone, sits still, afar —
he stares past the abyss, where void is found.

Once all this world was drowned in dark and mire.
The night had chained the mountains and the seas.
The woods and rivers slept and knew no fire,
no dawn had ever stirred among the trees.

They came from other worlds, the two as one —
the Beast, the Maker, light within his hand.
They rent the blind dark's veil till night was done,
and poured out life through grove and meadowland.

The sunset's embers dwindled to a gleam.
The Maker, silent, came the cliffward way,
a hot palm on the fur, as in a dream;
he said, "Farewell, my Keeper," walked away.

Around him gardens bloom and meadows wide,
the world exults, set free from every chain.
But he stares out into the void's blank tide,
and waits for him who crossed the worlds — in vain.

The ages pass; the ridges fall, decay;
the seas reshape, the rivers wander wide.
But none of all this beauty comes his way —
a keeper, locked forever, to abide.

He does not know the Maker's hidden aim.
A glassy longing froze within his eyes.
He waits, not knowing term, nor end, nor name,
and holds one warmth: that hand, beneath the skies.

The edge of worlds, where even time grows still.
Here lies the last frontier of all this world, to keep,
the giant beast sits waiting, mute and chill,
and in his soul there lies a void, immense and deep...

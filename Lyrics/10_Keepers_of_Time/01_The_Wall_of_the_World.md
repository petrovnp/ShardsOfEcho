# The Wall of the World

**Album:** Keepers of Time  
**Track:** 01  
**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

---

The Wall still stands by the Maker's design.
It runs along the boundary of all being.
The first hearts built it, every block and line,
born out of finest ether, past all seeing.

Already old, its stone is worn and tired —
it still recalls the oldest hammer.
Beyond it yawns a gulf where time's expired,
and through the cracks the dank, dead cold grows grimmer.

A dragon coils within the Wall's deep core —
named Keeper of the Wall in ages gone.
While he's alive, mankind lives evermore,
and dark beyond the brink cannot pass on.

A wondrous dream was his, that soared on high —
a boundless realm thrown open, vast and deep;
and there, above the earth, he learned to fly,
not knowing he lay lost in deepest sleep.

The centuries flow over him, a stream;
the ages turn, the generations ending.
And humankind began to fear his dream,
and darkened doubts arose, their gloom extending.

And then, one day, came men, and they drew near,
of those who never knew the Keeper sleeping,
and on the sleeper rained their blows, severe,
and broke his flight — his deathless dream, left weeping.

At once the cold bit sharper than before,
and stone itself recoiled, by darkness stricken;
a thousand fires went out, the whole world o'er,
and faceless waves came down, the dark to thicken.

The Maker heard that last and dreadful cry,
and in that breath, time froze and ceased its turning.
A hollow grief then filled his soul on high,
and on his heart a heavy weight lay, burning.

That howl passed through the bounds of every world,
and all creation's deep foundations shuddered;
the calm of ages, year on year, was hurled
to ruin; and the first breath, stilled, was smothered.

# The Ring of Time

When breakers smashed my frigate on the stone,
I washed ashore, half-dead, and dazed, and reeling,
and stumbled down the cliffs, alone, unknown —
then stopped, a sudden wonder through me stealing.

Before me yawned a narrow passage there,
where heaps of treasure lay, hushed and unspoken:
the diamonds scattered light through cold, dark air,
and golden coins blazed bright, their slumber broken.

Among the hoard, a skeleton, long dead,
sat hunched in shadow on a blackened boulder;
a wondrous ring, its quiet brightness shed,
still glimmered on the dead hand, growing colder.

I slid the ring off — in that breath, that flash,
the world around me shuddered, rocked, dissolving;
a strange new vision pierced my mind, and — crash —
time opened wide, its every lock revolving.

Now I could walk through every world and age,
appear, when needed, as an unseen shadow,
and plunder down the centuries, and rage,
and cast all former fears to depths gone hollow.

The years of triumph thundered past in pride,
my pirate empire spread, immense, and growing;
in all the splendor of those years that died,
my reckless fame rang out where seas were flowing.

But every time I called the gift to me,
a dread so deep it left me near to falling;
and often, in the night, I'd wake and see
a road back to that cavern, softly calling.

And so I chose to solve the dream at last;
I walked the island's path, in silence wending,
when from the dark a faint, low moaning passed —
a human sorrow, grief that knew no ending.

Each gleam of light here I have long since known,
but in the moonlight, there — a form, a stranger.
I move toward it, blade raised high, alone —
how, damn it all, did HE divine the danger?!

I drive the steel between his ribs and spine;
before my eyes that vision rises, towering —
I see his face at last — and it is MINE,
within my chest, a frozen dread devouring.

The cursèd gift had robbed me of my soul —
it languished in this cave, my own soul-keeper.
And only now I grasp the dreadful whole:
in losing her, I've met my death, the reaper.

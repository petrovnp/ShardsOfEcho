# The Lake of Fate

**Lyrics by:** Nickolay Petrov  
**Written:** 2026

Deep in a wood where pines grew dense and steep,
among the mossy boulders, stones, and roots,
there lay a lake of crystal, silent, clear, and deep,
it mirrored neither skies nor boughs nor shoots.

The legend says: a secret hides within —
it shows you who you might have grown to be.
Who once has looked finds peace forever thin —
your finest self's a fearsome thing to see.

One day a hunter galloped to the lake's still shore —
he swung down, proud and young, with haughty air.
He bent to drink — and froze, struck to the core.
A chief gazed back — fearless, beyond compare.

He snarled aloud and smote it with his fist —
the surface closed; the image stayed, unbent.
The hunter fled, blind, headlong, through the mist.
A chief he might have been — he dared not. Spent.

Then came a beauty, careless, bright, and proud —
a wanton who left burnt-out hearts behind.
But in the glass: a gentle wife, head bowed,
who rocked her son beside the porch, soft, kind.

She wept, and hid her face behind one trembling hand —
she could not bear that happiness, that gaze.
And turned away, and crossed the silent land.
To let love in — that is the fear that stays.

Then came a merchant — miserly and ill,
who lived friendless, knew no kind word, no grace.
But in the still clear water — bold of will,
a barefoot wanderer, with a sunlit face.

The miser shrank away and lost his speech.
But in the lake — that selfsame shape was plain.
He shuffled off, grown old past hope's last reach.
He hoarded all his days — a slave to gain.

And once, one summer evening, gold and still,
an old man came — a staff, a bundle tied.
He knelt beside the bank upon the grassy hill,
and gazed into the still pool, deep and wide.

And there was he himself — the same grey beard,
the selfsame features, line and brow and frame.
And softly o'er the water now he peered —
and smiled: he'd never fled his own dream's flame.

Deep in a wood where pines grew dense and steep,
among the mossy boulders, stones, and roots,
there darkens now a lake of mirror-water, deep,
reflecting neither skies nor boughs nor shoots.

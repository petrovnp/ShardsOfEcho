# The Dragon of Time

High in the peaks, among the endless snow,
a dragon slept in caverns underground.
Beneath him lay no gold, no hoard below —
but Time itself, which he had chained and bound.

And in the valley, where the slopes descend,
no soul has known a sorrow or a fear.
From oldest days they trusted, without end,
that death had never ventured even near.

They sang their songs around the firelight's glow,
they grew their bread and never counted years.
And he lay hidden in the peaks of snow,
in dreams that plunged through far, storm-lit frontiers.

He still recalled the sky — the furious flight,
the open meadows, freedom unsurpassed,
the thunder of his wings above the height,
the roar that shook the world with every blast.

Below, he's long forgotten, lost to mind;
the myths fall silent, telling him no more.
They never knew their peace was thus enshrined
by him — the dragon from the dawn of yore.

But should he rise, the flood of time will sweep
across it all, an unseen, drowning tide;
will age them in an instant, swift and deep,
and death will come for every soul inside.

Through rock he heard them, listening through the night,
their voices and their laughter round the flame.
He loved them, never sensing his own plight —
how, day by day, a deeper weakness came.

The hour will come — and silence, shuddering, breaks;
and time bursts through the ice and granite, fast.
Here he will die. And such will be the stakes —
for those who dwell below, at last.
